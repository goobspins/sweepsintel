# How I Made My First $50 From a Sweepstakes Casino in One Day

$52.37, ACH deposit, from some entity name I had to Google just to figure out which casino it even came from.

That was [MyPrize](/casinos/myprize). I signed up that morning after hearing about it on a podcast. Free signup, no deposit required. They hand you a chunk of Gold Coins and a small stack of Sweep Coins right away, I think it was 2.5 SC. Gold Coins are play money, worthless from a cash perspective. The Sweep Coins are what actually convert to real dollars.

You cant just cash them out immediately though. Theres a playthrough requirement, and MyPrize does this batched system where each SC credit has its own individual playthrough counter. Its kind of unique to them actually. So that 2.5 SC from signup needs to get wagered through slots before any of it becomes redeemable, which sounds annoying but in practice its just spinning at minimum bet for a while and letting the math do its thing.

I loaded up a slot called Pigsby at 0.10 SC per spin and just let it run while I was making lunch and answering emails. Between the signup bonus, the daily login bonus, and one small $4.99 Gold Coin purchase that came with bonus SC as a promo (thats how the purchase model works, you buy GC and they throw in SC on top), I had enough volume going through to clear the playthrough by that evening. My redeemable balance was sitting at 52-point-something. Hit redeem, and the ACH landed in my bank the next morning, which was honestly faster than I expected for a first redemption on a platform Id never used before.

The week after that I signed up for [Chumba](/casinos/chumba-casino) and [Global Poker](/casinos/global-poker) and thats when the whole model actually clicked for me. The daily bonuses alone generate redeemable SC over time without you ever buying anything. Global Poker in particular has zero promoban risk from what I can tell, and their gift card redemption is instant. Not fast. Instant. You click redeem and the gift card code is in your inbox before youve switched tabs.

Not every casino works that smoothly. Ive had redemptions take five days on other platforms, and I had one casino go completely silent on a support ticket for two weeks before finally processing it. Thats the whole reason I started tracking all of this, because the difference between a casino that pays out in 12 hours and one that ghosts you for a week is the difference between this being worth your time and it being a frustrating waste of it.

My first month across three casinos came out to about $180 in total redemptions on maybe 15-20 minutes of daily activity. Dont try to calculate an hourly rate on this or youll drive yourself crazy, its more like a coupon stacking hobby that occasionally deposits real money into your bank account. The numbers go up as you add more platforms and start figuring out which daily bonuses are actually worth claiming versus which ones look good on paper but dont convert to anything redeemable.
