# SweepsIntel — The Feel

> This document is not a spec. It's what the spec should feel like when it's built right.
> Read this first. Then read the spec. Build what's in between.

---

SweepsIntel opens like a cockpit at dawn. Dark. Warm. Everything where you left it.

The first thing you see is your momentum — a gradient bar, slow and heavy like mercury rising, already partway filled from yesterday's streak. The number beneath it isn't loud. It doesn't scream. It just sits there, quietly accumulating, the way a river carries things downstream. You set a goal once and now the bar fills toward it every single day without you thinking about it. Some days it's a crawl. Some weeks it floods. The weekly view smooths the noise into something that looks like a career.

Below the bar, your KPI cards breathe. Four of them, chosen by you, showing exactly the numbers that matter to your operation. Not dashboards for dashboards' sake — these are the vital signs of something you built. SC earned. Dollars moved. Purchases made. Redemptions pending. They update the moment you act. You claim a daily and the number ticks up before your finger leaves the mouse.

The casino list is the heartbeat. Each row is a living thing — a name, a timer, a state. Green means go. You tap, it saves, the row exhales. The button changes color when you switch modes — green for dailies, orange for adjustments, blue for spins — and there's something satisfying about that shift, like clicking a pen into a different color. You don't think about it. Your hands know the rhythm. Claim, claim, skip, claim, adjust, claim. Thirty casinos in four minutes. It should feel like shuffling cards.

When you scroll past your list, the world opens up. There's a casino you've never heard of, sitting in a spotlight, and it's telling you things nobody else would tell you. The wash game. The trap. The redemption speed. Not a sales pitch — a dossier. Someone did the homework and here it is, laid out clean, and beneath it a row of smaller cards with casinos you're sleeping on, each one just detailed enough to make you curious. The whole section whispers: *there's more here than you realize.*

Purchase entry shouldn't feel like accounting. It should feel like loading ammunition. You hit the buy button, punch in the cost, the SC comes back, and now that margin is sitting there — thirty percent, visible, real. The ledger catches it all with timestamps and the kind of quiet precision that lets you reconstruct any day from memory.

The entire application should feel like it was built by someone who uses it. Because it was. It should feel dense without feeling cluttered — every pixel earning its rent. Dark backgrounds. Muted borders. Accent colors that mean something: green is good, red is caution, amber is pending, blue is informational. Nothing decorative. Nothing that exists to look like a dashboard. It should look like a tool that's been used so much the edges are smooth.

Mobile isn't an afterthought but it's not the primary canvas either. This is a desktop tool that works on your phone when you need it. The phone version should feel like a field kit — same information, tighter, faster, no scrolling through chrome to get to the thing you came for.

When someone new opens SweepsIntel for the first time, they should feel like they just walked into a room where everyone knows something they don't — and instead of gatekeeping, the room hands them a chair and a drink and starts talking.
